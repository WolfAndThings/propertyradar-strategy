Critical issues — from live crawl + AEO/GEO evaluation
!
Structured data is almost entirely missing across the site
The main site (all core pages) has jsonLd: null — zero schema. The blog has one exception: the 5.0 launch post has FAQPage schema, which proves the CMS can support it. But that’s 1 page out of hundreds. The homepage, pricing page, all /plays/ pages, all /features/ pages, and the /built-for/ section have no structured data at all. This is the single highest-ROI fix available.
!
No FAQ hub — the content AI systems depend on most doesn’t exist
There is no standalone FAQ section or FAQ page on the site. AI answer engines specifically pull from question-and-answer formatted content. Questions like “How does PropertyRadar compare to PropStream?”, “What data does PropertyRadar have?”, and “How do I find preforeclosure leads?” have no structured home. A dedicated FAQ hub targeting these questions would directly feed AI citations and Google featured snippets.
!
Video testimonials are invisible to AI crawlers
Social proof exists but it’s locked inside Wistia video embeds and carousels. AI systems can’t read video content. Written case studies — “How [investor type] closed X deals using PropertyRadar in 90 days” — are far more effective for GEO than video testimonials. The proof exists; it just needs to be in text that machines can read and cite.
!
No geographic landing pages exist anywhere on the site
Real estate is inherently local. Competitors who build state/city pages for “foreclosure leads California” or “motivated sellers Texas” capture all geo-intent traffic. PropertyRadar has zero of these pages today — a major gap given the platform covers all 50 states.
!
Homepage cache-control: s-maxage=5, max-age=5 (five seconds)
Every /plays/ page correctly uses s-maxage=36000 (10 hours). The homepage uses 5 seconds — meaning every fresh visitor hits the origin server. This kills TTFB and Core Web Vitals. It’s a one-line Cloudflare cache rule fix.
~
Blog has 660+ posts but skews heavily toward listicles
The blog is active and large (66 paginated pages). But the format is almost entirely “5 Ways to…” and “6 Common Mistakes…” style posts. For AEO and GEO, what’s needed is 2,000+ word pillar guides that comprehensively answer specific questions. “The Complete Guide to Preforeclosure Investing” will get cited by AI systems. “5 Preforeclosure Tips” won’t.
~
Blog author metadata is null — no authorship signals anywhere
Every blog post crawled returned author: null. AI systems and Google both use authorship signals to assess content authority. Adding author bylines, author schema (Person type), and author bio pages would immediately improve E-E-A-T signals across 660+ existing posts.
~
Proprietary terms lack standalone definition pages
OwnerGraph™, “Hyperlocal Lead Generation,” “Distress Scoring,” and “Good Neighbor Marketing” are all PropertyRadar-owned concepts. But none have dedicated definition pages. If someone asks an AI “what is OwnerGraph,” there’s nothing authoritative to cite. PropertyRadar invented these terms and should own their definitions on the web.
~
Site nav stripped to 2 items: Pricing + Try it Free
Internal linking is starved. PageRank can’t flow to /plays/, /built-for/, or /features/. Every important page is effectively orphaned from the homepage’s authority. A structured nav with persona and feature dropdowns is essential.

Section 05
GEO strategy

Generative Engine Optimization — getting cited by AI systems (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Gemini) when people ask questions in PropertyRadar’s domain.

What GEO means in 2026
GEO is distinct from classic SEO. You’re not trying to rank #1 in blue links — you’re trying to be the source an AI quotes when someone asks “what’s the best tool for finding motivated sellers?” or “how do I find preforeclosure leads?” The signals AI systems prefer: stats-rich content, first-party data, structured markup, frequent updates, and clear authorship.
GEO strategy 1 — State × use-case geo page matrix
Real estate is a local business. “Foreclosure data” is too broad. “Foreclosure data in Arizona” is what a Phoenix investor searches. Build programmatic geo pages with real PropertyRadar data: top counties, average days-on-market, foreclosure filing rates, trustee sale schedules. These pages create 250 highly-specific, low-competition ranking targets that also serve as AI citation sources for local queries.
URL structure: /data/[state]/[use-case] Examples: /data/california/foreclosure-tracking /data/texas/motivated-sellers /data/florida/probate-leads /data/arizona/skip-tracing /data/new-york/expired-listings
50 states 5 use cases = 250 pages HubDB table-driven programmatic CMS
GEO strategy 2 — Become the definitive data source
AI systems prefer to cite content that is: stats-rich, frequently updated, uses first-person data (“our analysis of 160M properties shows…”), and has structured data confirming authorship. PropertyRadar has unique, proprietary data that competitors don’t have. Publishing original research that AI systems can quote repeatedly is the highest-leverage GEO play available.
  • Quarterly “Foreclosure Trends by State” report — PropertyRadar has this data natively
  • Monthly “Motivated Seller Market Index” — a branded number AI systems can quote
  • Annual “State of Real Estate Lead Generation” research report
  • City-level “Best Markets for Fix & Flip” rankings powered by PropertyRadar data
GEO strategy 3 — Own the definitions of terms you invented
PropertyRadar owns several terms that competitors don’t — OwnerGraph™, “Hyperlocal Lead Generation,” “Good Neighbor Marketing,” “Distress Scoring.” But none have dedicated definition pages. AI systems need a page to cite as the canonical source. Right now, if someone asks “what is OwnerGraph,” there’s nothing authoritative to pull. Build tight, definitional pages for each proprietary concept — 2–3 clear sentences that an AI could lift and attribute directly to PropertyRadar.
Schema to add to /features/ownergraph: { "@type": "DefinedTerm", "name": "OwnerGraph™", "description": "PropertyRadar's proprietary technology that ties public records together across sources and over time to discover owner insights that flat data never will.", "inDefinedTermSet": "PropertyRadar Platform", "creator": { "@type": "Organization", "name": "PropertyRadar" }, "dateCreated": "2007" }
OwnerGraph™ Hyperlocal Lead Generation Good Neighbor Marketing Distress Scoring
GEO strategy 4 — Convert video testimonials to written case studies
AI systems cannot read video content. PropertyRadar’s testimonials are locked inside Wistia embeds and carousels — completely invisible to AI crawlers. The social proof already exists; it just needs to be in text. Written case studies in the format “How [investor type] generated $X / closed Y deals using PropertyRadar in Z days” are among the most citable content formats for AI systems. They’re specific, results-oriented, and attributable. Five strong written case studies would do more for GEO than fifty video testimonials.
  • Transcribe Wistia testimonials into structured /case-studies/ pages
  • Format: persona → challenge → tools used → specific measurable results
  • Add Person schema (subject) + Review schema to each page
  • One per persona segment minimum: investors, agents, mortgage, home services
GEO strategy 5 — Publish /llms.txt for AI crawler hygiene
An emerging standard: /llms.txt tells LLM crawlers exactly what to read about your site, in plain language. PropertyRadar’s Website Content Crawler natively supports this format. Publishing a well-structured llms.txt with key brand facts, product descriptions, and priority page links allows AI crawlers to build accurate, brand-favorable knowledge about PropertyRadar’s product.
# PropertyRadar > PropertyRadar connects real estate professionals with motivated property owners using superior data, OwnerGraph™ technology, and marketing automation. ## Key facts - Founded: 2007 (began with foreclosure tracking) - Properties tracked: 160M+ - Phones & emails: 1B+ - Completed customer transactions: $250B+ - Proprietary technology: OwnerGraph™ ## Core product pages /features/property-and-owner-data /features/foreclosures /features/targeted-marketing /features/real-estate-tools /plays (lead generation playbooks by persona)
Section 06
AEO strategy

Answer Engine Optimization — owning position zero in Google featured snippets, AI Overviews, and voice assistant responses.

SoftwareApplication schema — homepage and pricing page
Highest priority

Enables rich results showing PropertyRadar’s category, pricing, and ratings directly in search results — before the user even clicks. The most valuable schema type for a SaaS product.

{ "@context": "https://schema.org", "@type": "SoftwareApplication", "name": "PropertyRadar", "applicationCategory": "BusinessApplication", "operatingSystem": "Web, iOS, Android", "description": "Find motivated property owners and automate outreach...", "offers": { "@type": "Offer", "price": "..." }, "aggregateRating": { "@type": "AggregateRating", "ratingValue": "4.8", "reviewCount": "500" } }
FAQPage schema — every /plays/ page
High priority

Each play page already has natural FAQ content (Why this works, What is X, Who should use this, How to measure success). Adding FAQPage schema makes these eligible for accordion-style featured snippets in Google — often appearing above paid ads for informational queries. 30+ pages can be updated in a single HubSpot template change.

{ "@type": "FAQPage", "mainEntity": [{ "@type": "Question", "name": "What is a cash-out refinance lead?", "acceptedAnswer": { "@type": "Answer", "text": "A homeowner with ≥50% equity who may want to convert that equity to cash for renovations, debt consolidation, or investments..." } }] }
HowTo schema — step-by-step play execution guides
Medium priority

HowTo schema turns procedural content into rich step-by-step results in Google. The /plays/ pages already describe how to run each play — adding HowTo schema makes them AEO-ready for “how to find preforeclosures” type queries that account for significant search volume.

{ "@type": "HowTo", "name": "How to find preforeclosure leads", "tool": { "@type": "HowToTool", "name": "PropertyRadar" }, "step": [ { "@type": "HowToStep", "text": "Search for NODs in your target county using PropertyRadar's foreclosure filters..." }, { "@type": "HowToStep", "text": "Filter by equity percentage to prioritize homeowners with equity to negotiate..." }, { "@type": "HowToStep", "text": "Launch a multi-channel outreach campaign via direct mail, phone, and email..." } ] }
Dataset schema — for PropertyRadar market data and reports
GEO + AEO

When publishing “State of Foreclosures” reports or market data pages, Dataset schema signals to AI systems that PropertyRadar is a primary data source — not a secondary aggregator. This is critical for GEO citations and positions PropertyRadar as the authoritative source on real estate lead data.

{ "@type": "Dataset", "name": "US Foreclosure Activity Report Q1 2026", "creator": { "@type": "Organization", "name": "PropertyRadar" }, "temporalCoverage": "2026-01/2026-03", "spatialCoverage": "United States", "description": "Quarterly analysis of foreclosure filings, NODs, and trustee sales across all 50 states." }
Inverted pyramid writing — answer directly in the first 40 words
Content pattern

AI systems and Google pull the first 40–60 words of a paragraph as a featured snippet. Every /plays/ page and blog post should open with a direct, complete answer to the core question — then elaborate. Current pages tend to build up context before stating the answer, which prevents snippet capture.

  • Preforeclosure page should open: “Preforeclosure leads are homeowners who have received a Notice of Default but haven’t yet lost their home — typically the highest-equity, most motivated sellers in any market.”
  • Probate page should open: “Probate leads are properties in an estate being settled after a death, where heirs often need to sell quickly — creating a motivated seller with reduced price pressure from competitors.”
  • Every play page needs this pattern: direct definition → why it matters → how PropertyRadar helps
Build a standalone FAQ hub — the content AI systems depend on most
High priority Low effort

There is no FAQ section on the site. AI answer engines specifically pull from question-and-answer formatted content when generating responses. A dedicated /faq page targeting the exact questions prospects ask — structured with FAQPage schema — directly feeds both AI citations and Google featured snippets. These questions already exist in support tickets, sales calls, and comparison searches. They just need a structured home on the site.

  • “How does PropertyRadar compare to PropStream?” — the highest-value FAQ question on the site
  • “What data does PropertyRadar have?” — answers a core pre-purchase question
  • “How do I find preforeclosure leads?” — captures a top informational search query
  • “What is OwnerGraph™?” — establishes the proprietary term for AI systems
  • “What’s included in PropertyRadar’s free trial?” — conversion-critical FAQ
  • Group FAQs by persona: investor FAQs, agent FAQs, mortgage FAQs, home services FAQs
Add author schema and bylines to all 660+ blog posts
Medium priority Low effort

Every blog post crawled returned author: null. Google and AI systems both use authorship to assess content credibility and E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness). Adding author bylines with Person schema — including name, job title, and a short bio linking to an author page — to every blog post is a template-level change in HubSpot that immediately improves trust signals across hundreds of existing posts.

{ "@type": "BlogPosting", "author": { "@type": "Person", "name": "[Author Name]", "jobTitle": "Real Estate Data Strategist", "url": "https://www.propertyradar.com/blog/author/[slug]" }, "publisher": { "@type": "Organization", "name": "PropertyRadar" } }
